Chart Analysis

When we look at scatterplot, we should be able to describe the association we see between the variables. A quick description of the association in a scatterplot should always include a description of the form, direction, and strength of the association, along with the presence of any outliers.

  • Form: Is the association linear or nonlinear?
  • Direction: Is the association positive or negative?
  • Strength: Does the association appear to be strong, moderately strong, or weak?

The x-axis data for marital status (single) came from the Census Bureau's American Community Survey, and the y-axis (drink alcohol) is from the Census Bureau's Behavior Risk Factor Surveillance System. Prior to plotting the chart, a correlation check was performed in excel using the CORREL function and that yielded a value of 0.27 which translates to a weak correlation. A value of -0.5 to 0.5 indicates a moderate correlation, so there are better choices besides single and drinks alcohol, but after going through several trials, this pair had one of the highest correlation numbers I found at first. Looking at the scatter plot, there is a non-linear grouping, but I would say it is an upward and positive association with one outlier. The majority of data points are above 40 percent on the y-axis, and between 27 and 40 percent on the x-axis, so there is weak to moderate association of single people consuming alcohol which makes sense to me. Is this result just random chance or really representative?
